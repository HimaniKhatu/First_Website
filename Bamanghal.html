Bamanghal

♦About Bamanghal:

Bamanghal, also known locally as Baman Ghal (बामणघळ), is a fascinating natural sea gorge located near the coastal village of Hedvi in the Ratnagiri district of Maharashtra, India. Just about 3 kilometers from the famous Dashbhuja Ganesh Temple in Guhagar, this spot is known for its dramatic interaction between sea and rock. The gorge itself is a narrow fissure in the rocky coastline, roughly one meter wide and three to four meters deep. During high tide, seawater forcefully gushes into this narrow passage and shoots upwards, creating a powerful and visually striking water jet that can reach heights of 13 to 15 feet. This natural display is most spectacular during the monsoon season, when the tides are high and the sea is especially active. To reach Bamanghal, visitors typically walk about 300 meters along a rugged, rocky path from the nearby Uma Maheshwar Temple at Hedvi Beach. The area is relatively undeveloped, so it's recommended to wear sturdy footwear and exercise caution, especially when wet. Although basic amenities are available in Hedvi and Guhagar, Bamanghal remains largely untouched, offering a raw and awe-inspiring experience of nature's force for those who venture there.

Vyadeshwar Temple

♦About Vyadeshwar Temple:

The Vyadeshwar Temple, located in Guhagar in the Ratnagiri district of Maharashtra, is an ancient and revered shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, who is worshipped here as Vyadeshwar.Architecturally, the temple is built in the traditional Panchayatan style, featuring a central sanctum dedicated to Shiva and four subsidiary shrines for Lord Ganesha, Goddess Ambika (Parvati), Lord Vishnu with Goddess Lakshmi, and Lord Surya. A majestic statue of Nandi, the divine bull and vehicle of Shiva, faces the main shrine. The temple complex also includes idols of Garuda and Hanuman near the east-facing entrance, and has multiple entry points from the east, west, and south.Vyadeshwar Temple is the focal point of various Hindu festivals such as Maha Shivaratri, Tripurari Poornima, Ashadhi and Kartik Ekadashi, Vijayadashami, and Shimga, drawing many devotees during these celebrations.
